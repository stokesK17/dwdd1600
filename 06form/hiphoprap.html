Artists

  1. 2Pac

    2Pac (Tupac Shakur) is often regarded as one of the greatest and most influential rappers of all time. His powerful lyrics about social justice, struggles in inner-city life, and his own personal demons made him a voice for the voiceless. With albums like All Eyez on Me and Me Against the World, 2Pac became an icon of both the West Coast rap scene and hip-hop at large.

  2. The Notorious B.I.G.

    The Notorious B.I.G. (Biggie Smalls) was a master storyteller whose influence on hip-hop continues to be profound. His smooth flow, vivid imagery, and compelling narratives about street life are epitomized in his albums Ready to Die and Life After Death. Biggie's contributions to East Coast rap and his status as one of the genreâ€™s legends remain unmatched.

    Jay-Z's career has spanned decades, with each album showcasing his evolution as both an artist and businessman. From his early work on Reasonable Doubt to his later, more reflective albums like 4:44, Jay-Z's impact on hip-hop is undeniable.   4. Nicki Minaj

    Nicki Minaj is one of the most successful and influential female artists in hip-hop history. Her albums, including Pink Friday and The Pinkprint, have cemented her place in rap's modern era, where she continues to push the boundaries of the genre.
